As part of my PhD research, I developed the Knee-Lax system, an innovative robotic tool designed to assess the laxity of ex-vivo knee joints, comparing native conditions with post-TKA scenarios.

### Project Details
* **Hardware Integration:** Utilized a collaborative KUKA LBR Med 14 R820 robot combined with an NDI Polaris Vega optical tracker.
* **Control System:** Designed custom algorithms to apply specific forces and torques (up to 6 DoF) to the knee joint, ensuring precise measurement of varus/valgus and internal/external rotations.
* **Outcome:** The system successfully quantified laxity variations across different flexion angles, providing objective data to improve implant design and surgical techniques.
