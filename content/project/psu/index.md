Laboratory equipment can be prohibitively expensive. To equip my personal electronics workspace, I reverse-engineered and upcycled a discarded 450W computer ATX power supply, converting it into a versatile programmable bench tool.

### Project Details
* **Hardware Hacking:** Bypassed the ATX motherboard protection circuits to force the unit to power on independently.
* **Voltage Regulation:** Integrated step-up (boost) and step-down (buck) DC-DC converters to provide variable, programmable voltage and current outputs alongside the standard fixed ATX rails (3.3V, 5V, 12V).
* **Enclosure & UI:** Wired external binding posts, master switches, and digital voltage/current displays for real-time monitoring and safe operation.
