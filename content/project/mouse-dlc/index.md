Based on state-of-the-art neurobehavioral frameworks, this project, carried out in collaboration with the neurophysiology laboratory of National Research Council of Pisa, IT, involved the deployment of DeepLabCut, a markerless pose estimation tool based on transfer learning, to track and quantify facial keypoints in murine models.

### Project Details
* **Technology:** Utilized DeepLabCut (built on ResNet architectures) to automate the tracking of specific facial landmarks (e.g., ears, nose, eyes).
* **Application:** Facilitated objective, automated analysis of pain or emotional states in mice, replacing subjective manual coding with highly reproducible kinematic data.
* **GUI development:** The software has been integrated in a dedicated GUI to facilitate the data acquisition.
