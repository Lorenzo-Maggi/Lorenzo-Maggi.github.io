As part of my PhD research, I worked on integrating wearable tactile displays with a rehabilitative hand exoskeleton to enhance immersivity and realism in Virtual Reality (VR) environments. The goal was to convey both force and motion cues, providing kinesthetic feedback for recognizing the softness of virtual objects.

### My Technical Contribution
* **Unity 3D Physics:** Developed the core algorithm for the real-time estimation of virtual object indentation during user interaction.
* **Kinesthetic Integration:** Interfaced the Unity physics engine with the hand exoskeleton hardware based on Raspberry Pi, ensuring synchronized force feedback delivery based on the virtual collision depth.

### Academic Impact & Mentorship
* **Mentorship:** The indentation estimation system and the VR architecture served as the foundational technical baseline for a master thesis.
* **Research Output:** The preliminary results of this integration were submitted as a conference abstract, successfully demonstrating improvements in users' ability to recognize softness differences between virtual objects compared to using tactile feedback alone.
